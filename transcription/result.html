1 General rules

1 § - Scope of the law

In accordance to Verfassung § 12, an association can beestablished by a citizen to promote a shared specific idea. Anassociation shall not be against the law or good manners.

Any association is subject to this law.

2 § - Restrictions

This law shall not be applied if the association is intended togenerate profit or other corresponding economic benefit for itsmembers, or if the idea of the association is economic in generalsense.

Any association that intends to form a hierarchy of groups withinitself, or that intends to arm its members for paramilitarymeans, is prohibited. An association related to firearms or thepossession of other dangerous objects and substances, shallrequire an approval from the Federal Court.

An association must have a name that is in accordance to goodmanners and does not infringe copyrighted names of otherorganisations.

Members of an association are not personally responsible for theassociation. However, if a board member of the association causesdamages to the association through their actions, the associationcan demand them to cover the damages.

3 § - Finance

An association may generate revenue only in ways that are definedto serve the idea of the association. All generated revenue shallbe considered as assets of the association and may not be givenfor personal use to any member of the association.

2 Establishment

4 § - Charter

An association is established with a charter which definesmembership, financing, intentions and founding. Charter must besigned by at least two founding members and it must be verifiedby the State Court. Charter shall not be classified in any way.

5 § - Registration

All associations shall be registered in State Courts.Registration requires a legal and applicable charter, thepresence of at least two founding members and a witness. Inconjunction with the registration, a financial secretary must benamed for the association to hold its account.

Registration can require a fee, but it must not exceed 100 €.

3 Membership

6 § - Members

Prerequisites of the membership of an association shall bedefined in the association's charter. Membership recuirementsmust be in accordance to law and no one shall be discriminatedwhen processing applications for membership.

An association shall have the right to denounce any member'smembership in the association, if the reasons have been definedin the charter. In other instances, a member must be warned atleast a week before the end of their membership.

7 § - Member registry

An association must keep a registry of its members, includingtheir legal names and identification codes. The registry must beverified by the State Court and it must be handledconfidentially.

In accordance to Verfassung § 69, the member registry shall beheld by the State Court. The association must inform the court ofany new members, so that they may be added to the registry. Theassociation shall be required to destroy any own reports ofindividual members' identification codes.

8 § - Acquiring membership

All natural persons with citizenship and native legal personsliving or operating in the State in which an association wasfounded, shall be granted the right to apply for membership inthe association. Acceptance of new members must be based on legalreasons.

A citizen from another State shall be able to apply for themembership of an association, if the association has allowed thisin their charter. State citizenship can be verified by the StateCourt and then reported to the association, without includingsensitive and specifically accurate information of location oraddress.

Membership can be made subject to payment. The payment intervaland amount can be defined in the charter and it must be the samefor all members. If a member continuously fails to pay for theirmembership, or has not paid for it within the last two months,the association can denounce the membership with a one-sidednotice.

9 § - Denouncing membership

A member may denounce their membership themselves. An associationmay denounce the membership of one their members for reasonsdefined in § 8, or if the member has commited a crime that isespecially harmful for the association or its reputation.

If the reason for denouncing a membership by the association isnot financial, a notice of the denounciation must be submitted tothe member in two weeks before it becomes valid.

A person whose membership has been denounced by the associationcan apply for new membership in no earlier than a year, unlessotherwise stated in the association's charter or another relevantdocument.

4 Authority

10 § - Decision making

An association may define its intentions, ideas, purposes, andother actions in decisions issue with the collective authority ofits members.

All members of the association shall have the right to vote ondecisions in meetings held at least twice a year. Decisions arebinding when at least half of all members are present in themeeting and when the decision has receive an absolute majority ofvotes.

The members of an association shall choose a board amongthemselves. The board shall have three permanent seats:

• treasurer; to access and manage the association's assets.

• bookkeeper; to access and manage the association's documents.

• chairman; to organise meetings and the operation of theassociation.

Other members of the board can be named in the charter. The boardmay not have more seats than half the amount of members, unlessthe amount of members is under six persons.

11 § - Access to decision making

All members of an association must be sent a notice of anupcoming meeting at least a week before the meeting takes place.A list of affairs to be voted on must be included. The right tovote can be transferred to a representative within theassociation, if the representative is informed of votingdecisions that are to be taken as a representative.

12 § - Election of board members

Members of the board of an association must be elected in ameeting. Elections must be held at least every other year.Members of the board must be members of the association and notunder the notice of denouncement of their membership.

13 § - Board proposals

The board of an association shall have the decide which affairsare to be voted on in meetings. However, all members of theassociation must be granted the chance to speak in meetings andthus propose matters, that they deem important for theassociation.

The board shall have the right to make official proposals thatcan be turned into binding policies of the association.

The process of discussing affairs in meetings can be definedfurther in the charter or another relevant binding document.

5 Dissolution

14 § - Dissolving an association

The board of an association may terminate the legal existence ofthe association and initiate the dissolution of it, by having thedecision approved by members of the association in a meeting.

The decision to dissolve an association shall not be enforced, ifthe association is under financial scrutiny by a court to payfines or damages, or if the association is in debt by othermeans.

The decision to dissolve an association must rest for at least amonth and all members must be granted the right to appeal thedecision. Three appeals at most shall be allowed, and if all ofthem are overturned, the dissolution shall occure immediately.

15 § - Legal existence

The dissolution of an association is legally enforced once theState Court has verified it. Once the association is dissolved,its member registry held by the Court shall be classified assecret, and it must not be accessible to former members of theassociation.

All assets of an association, at the moment of dissolution, shallbe subject to private profit tax, as defined in Tax Law. Oncetaxed, the net assets shall be shared between the former membersevenly. Such provisions shared to members shall be subject topersonal tax, as defined in Tax Law.

6 Status and liabily

16 § - The status of an association

A registered association shall be deemed a legal person asreferred to in Verfassung § 73. Members of the association arenot personally responsible for it, even as members of the board.

A registered association can be represented in courts and inother interactions with authorities, by the chairman of theboard.

17 § - Liability to pay damages

An association is liable for damages caused by its own actions.If the assets of the association are not sufficient to cover thedamages, membership payments shall be diverted to cover them. Ifthere are no enforced membership payments or financialrequirements for membership, all members shall pay 100 € monthlyto cover the damages, until at least half of them are covered.
