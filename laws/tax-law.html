1 Basis of the law

1 § - Intentions

This law intents to provide funding for all federal administration. Federal funding is not limited to taxes. Taxes for both States and the Federation are collected together, and shared later appropriately according to what is decreed.

Taxes shall be issued only according to what is decreed in this law. The purpose of Tax Law is to provide both funding to the federation, and protection from unjust financial exploitation to persons living or operating in Stutzland.

2 § - Constitutional grounds

This law implements Verfassung § 73, § 74, and § 75. The federal exclusive right to issue taxes shall be from the constitution and this law.

If taxation, that infringes the constitution, occures, shall the Federation pay back all the taxable subjects that were wrongly taxed.

3 § - Progressive taxation

This law, its derivatives, and directives derived from it, shall be based on the principle of progressive taxation, unless otherwise specifically stated.

Progressive taxation implies, that the percentage of taxes paid shall increase in conjunction with the taxable subject’s wealth. Besides, taxes must always be presented as percentages calculated from associated wealth, instead of fixed sums, except for minor instances when otherwise stated.

4 § - Ministry of Finances

In accordance to Verfassung § 39, the Ministry of Finances has the authority to manage federal finances under the supervision of the Federal Government and the Federal Parliament. In this law, when referring to a directive to be decreed, the directive in question shall be issued by the Ministry of Finances, unless otherwise stated.

2 Federal Income Register

5 § - Purpose

In accordance to Verfassung § 73, all income or revenue earned by taxable subjects, including legal and natural persons, must be reported to Federal Income Register, abbreviated as FIR, managed and documented by the Ministry of Finances. Individual income reports shall be collected quarterly every year, and they must include information of monthly personal assets and their changes. This information must be classified as confidential.

Federal Income Register shall be used as the primary source of information by the authorities, when processing affairs related to incomes or assets. It is the duty of the Register to conduct information gathering, database managing, and other relevant efforts all for the Ministry of Finances.

6 § - Organisation

Federal Income Register is a federal office within the Ministry of Finances, supervised by an assigned State Secretary. FIR shall have the right to view accounts from native banks to fulfill its duties.

Individual income reports must be inserted to the primary register of private assets (PRPA), and a backup must be copied to the secondary register of private assets (SRPA). The reports must specify the earner or owner of the associated assets with the owner’s identification code, contact information, and address. The source of the assets must also be specified.

The Register shall have an executive accountant and a reviewing accountant. It is the responsibility of the executive accountant to keep the primary register of private assets up to date, while it is the responsibility of the reviewing accountant to ensure, that the primary and the secondary registers of private assets are equal and taxation resolutions are legal and up to standards.

Accountants must be given the appropriate tools to conduct their work, and access to classified information that is deemed important for their work. Their position as officials shall be in accordance to Law on Federal Administration.

3 Treasury of Federal Revenue

7 § - Purpose

In accordance to Verfassung § 73, taxes shall be collected by the Treasury of Federal Revenue, abbreviated as TEFER or the Treasury. TEFER shall be an administrational office and authority under the supervision of the Ministry of Finances, and it must have unrestricted access to Federal Income Registry, to conduct calculations for resolutions of individual taxation.

It is the duty of the Treasury to conduct taxation, supervise taxation, exact taxes, and account taxes, all for the Ministry of Finances. The Treasury must fulfill the tasks assigned by the Ministry, that concern reporting, planning, or controlling taxation.

8 § - Organisation

Treasury of Federal Revenue is a federal office within the Ministry of Finances, supervised by an assigned State Secretary. The Treasury shall have an executive accountant and a reviewing accountant. It is the responsibility of the executive accountant to draft tax resolutions, while it is the responsibility of the reviewing accountant to make sure that the resolutions are correct, and that they comply with the existing legislation.

Accountants must be given the appropriate tools to conduct their work, and access to classified information that is deemed important for their work. Their position as officials shall be in accordance to Law on Federal Administration.

9 § - Tax resolutions

Tax resolution is a binding decision of the Treasury of Federal Revenue, that dictates the amount of taxes the receiver of the resolution must pay. A resolution shall be annual, and their receiver must be informed of it before the following annual federal budget is approved. A tax resolution must specify the following information:

  • the person the resolution concerns
  • the person’s contact information
  • the person’s identification code
  • the person’s address
  • amount of taxes to be paid
  • deadline of payment

All taxable subjects must be granted at least one, but no more than three months of time to pay their taxes. Failure to pay taxes shall be punishable. If a taxable subject’s income or assets change in a way that affects their taxation, it is their duty to inform either the Treasury directly or the Federal Income Register. Failure to produce accurate information when mandated shall be punishable.

4 Value added tax

10 § - Subjects

All revenue generated from selling buying goods or services in trade between consumers and businesses, is subject to value added tax, abbreviated as VAT. Prices of such goods and services must be displayed with the tax included. It is the responsibility of the seller to report their revenue to the Federal Income Register.

Unofficial trade between private natural persons, not concerning business or trade as means of living, is not subject to VAT. Official trade between private natural persons, not concerning business or trade as means of living, is not subject to VAT when the value of the trade does not exceed fifty thousand euros.

11 § - Value

As an exception to § 3 of this law, value added tax shall be 20,00 per cent in value, as a flat tax.

Value added tax shall be paid once annually.

12 § - Exceptions

Value of trade between private legal persons shall not be subject to value added tax, but it must be reported. Employment contracts shall not be considered as trade between private legal persons in any instance.

5 Private profit tax

13 § - Subjects

All income as profit earned from owning shares of companies and corporations, and all profit earned by legal persons from selling goods or services, shall be subject to private profit tax. Capital investment in company or corporation shares shall be deducted from the taxable income. In case of value added tax applying, it shall be deducted first.

Profit earned from owning shares shall be subject to tax once it has been realised. Shares as financing instruments shall not be taxed until realised.

14 § - Value

As an exception to § 3 of this law, value added tax shall be 17,50 per cent in value, as a flat tax.

Private profit tax shall be paid once annually.

15 § - Exceptions

Profit earned by companies and corporations, with federal percentage of shares above 50,00 %, shall not be subject to private profit tax for a portion that equals the amount of federal ownership.

6 Personal tax

16 § - Subjects

All income earned by natural persons as wages, compensations, or other corresponding fees, from employment contracts or from being elected into a public office, shall be subject to personal tax. Thus, it is the intention of the tax to be applicable to all personal income unrelated to trade or businesses.

In case of employment contracts, it shall be the responsibility of the employer to deduct taxes from their employees and report them to the Federal Income Register. Associated employees must be informed of the deduction and it must be stated in the employment contract. In case of being taxed more than should have, the employee must be returned the excessive taxes.

17 § - Value

Personal tax conforms to § 3 of this law. Taxpayers shall be divided into classes by income, from lowest to highest. The difference in income between the lowest and highest class must be so, that the highest income class must be at least two times higher in value, than the lowest income class.

Personal tax shall not be lower than 1,00 per cent, nor higher than 50,00 per cent in any income class. Income classes and their taxation percentages shall be defined further in a directive.

Personal income shall be taxed once annually.

18 § - Exceptions

Certain household expenses shall be made available for deduction in taxation. These expenses shall include commute trips and self-financed meals at work. The associated employee may inform the Federal Income Register of such expenses and if verified, they shall be deducted from taxable personal income.

7 Vehicle tax

19 § - Subjects

Subjects to vehicle tax are persons owning registered vehicles, that have been allowed for road use. Vehicle tax shall be paid once annually and failure to pay it shall not result in fines or detention, but in prohibition to use the vehicle on public roads.

20 § - Value

As an exception to § 3 of this law, vehicle tax shall be a fixed sum of 120 euros.

21 § - Exceptions

Vehicles owned by public offices, such as the federal administration, are not to be subjects to vehicle tax.

8 Fees comparable to taxes

22 § - Health insurance payment

In accordance to Verfassung § 15, the Federal State is bound to provide healthcare to all its citizens. This shall be, at minimum, financed with a health insurance payment. All natural persons above the age of majority, earning personal income as meant in § of this law, are subject to health insurance payment.

Formation of the value of the health insurance payment shall be defined further in a separate law.

23 § - Pension insurance payment

In accordance to Verfassung § 16, the Federal State is bound to provide pensions as defined in the paragraph. These pensions shall be, at minimum, financed with a pension insurance payment. All natural persons above the age of majority, earning personal income as meant in § of this law, are subject to pension insurance payment.

Formation of the value of the pension insurance payment shall be defined further in a separate law.

9 State taxes

24 § - State property tax

The States of Stutzland shall be allowed to tax private properties for their landuse, once annually. The tax can be either a fixed sum, or in relation to the area of the property. State Parliaments shall be allowed to decide the value of the tax in their own region.
